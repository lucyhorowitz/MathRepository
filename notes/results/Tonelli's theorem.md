Let $(X,\Sigma,\mu)$ and $(Y,T,\nu)$ be [[Ïƒ-finite]] [[measure space|measure spaces]] and $f:X\times Y \to [0,\infty]$ be [[measurable function|measurable]], where $X\times Y$ is endowed with the [[product measure]]. Then $$\int_{X\times Y} f(x,y)\text d(x,y) = \int_X\left(\int_Yf(x,y)\text dy\right)\text dx = \int_Y\left(\int_X f(x,y)\text dx\right)\text dy.$$ See [[Fubini's theorem#Theorem MATH 20900 version|Fubini's theorem]].
